# Mentor Review — Problem Statement Selection

**Date:** June 2026
**Review:** Mentor Session 01
**Outcome:** All shortlisted statements rejected — new statement provided by mentor

---

## Previous Shortlisted Statements — Rejected

Our team initially shortlisted 4 problem statements from
36 industrial problems. After mentor review, all 4 were
rejected.

**Reason for rejection:**
- Statements were too common and already heavily researched
- Not aligned with current industry and research trends
- Lacked innovation and technical depth

---

## How the New Statement Was Selected

The mentor directly provided a new research direction
combining two emerging areas:

1. **Composite filament development** — creating a new
   material by blending already available filaments
2. **Machine Learning** — using ML to predict and optimize
   the mechanical properties of the new composite

This combination was selected because:
- Composite materials in 3D printing is an active research area
- ML-based material optimization is highly relevant in 2025–26
- It combines mechanical engineering with data science
- Strong scope for testing, analysis, and publication

---

## Final Approved Problem Statement

**Development of a New Composite Filament for FDM 3D Printing
Using ML-Based Property Optimization**

A new composite filament will be created by combining two or
more existing filaments (such as PLA, ABS, PETG, or
fiber-reinforced variants). Machine Learning will be used to
predict and optimize mechanical properties — tensile strength,
flexibility, and surface quality — based on blend ratio and
printing parameters.

---

## Key Areas of the Project

| Area | Details |
|---|---|
| Material | Composite of existing filaments |
| Process | FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printing |
| ML Role | Predict and optimize mechanical properties |
| Input variables | Blend ratio, print speed, temperature, layer height |
| Output / Target | Tensile strength, flexibility, surface quality |
